One-Pan Gnocchi with Sausage and Spinach

When you are starving and need Italian comfort food without a lot of fuss, this is your baby. It’s sooooo good. It takes about 20 minutes, start to finish. Serves 4.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lbs Italian sausage (bulk, no casings)
  • 1 (16oz) package fresh potato gnocchi
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can Italian flavored diced tomatos with juices
  • 1/4 cup low sodium chicken broth
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded parmesan cheese

Directions

  1. In a large nonstick skillet, add oil and heat over medium high until hot. Add onion and garlic and saute until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  2. Add sausage; stir and break it up into pieces. Continue cooking until nicely browned. Don’t drain.
  3. Add uncooked gnocchi. Stir for 1 minute, gently breaking up any clumps.
  4. Stir in tomatoes with juices and chicken broth.
  5. Cover and cook on medium heat for 5 minutes or just until gnocchi is fluffy and cooked through.
  6. Turn off heat. Add spinach and pepper. Stir until spinach leaves are wilted.
  7. Sprinkle with cheeses and cover for 3-5 minutes or until cheese is melted. Serve immediately.

Notes

Make it meatless: Simply skip the sausage and add another veggie instead. Sub veggie broth for chicken broth.
